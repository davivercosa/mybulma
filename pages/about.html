About us!

Meet SMART,

a group of trail advocates that works together to connect trails locally, regionally and to neighboring states. SMART is organized as a 501(c)(3) non-profit and includes representatives from 11 counties in southeastern Minnesota:

Our counties:

  • Dodge
  • Fillmore
  • Freeborn
  • Goodhue

Our primary objectives are

  1. To provide a forum for trail related discussions and recommendations.
  2. To enhance the southeastern Minnesota trails system through educational materials, such as brochures, websites, and maps.
  3. To assure connectivity locally, regionally, and to neighboring states.
  4. To explore existing and seek new trail funding sources and partnerships from:
    •      - Private and corporate entities
    •      - Federal, state, regional, and local units of government
    •      - City park and recreation departments
    •      - State highway and county road right-of-way partnerships
  5. To develop cooperative relationships and work in concert with private and corporate organizations and with federal, state, regional, and local units of government.

What does SMART do?

Representatives from the 11 member counties, the Department of Natural Resources (DNR), the Minnesota Department of Transportation (Mn/DOT), and trail advocates attend quarterly meetings in Rochester and other locations in southeast Minnesota. The meetings include a scheduled speaker on a trail-related subject, updates from Mn/DOT and DNR representatives, and sharing of updates on regional trail activities
